Maps and History

Images of Vodou Practices

Images from Citizen Sketcher, Field Museum, Stephanie Keith

Map of diffusion

A map displaying the diffusion of Vodou from Africa over to Haiti and New Orleans during the triangular slave trade.

Map of current practitioners

An unbiased map displaying the amount of people who practice Vodou around the world.

Vodou Temple

Factual layout and map of the Vodou temple in New Orleans.

Woman charged in Vodou ritual

A video on the trial of a woman charged with harming her child because she was possessed by a spirit. This video is biased against the Vodou religion by assuming that Vodou is a religion full of harmful ideals and rituals, instead of acceptance and goodwill.

Christian and Vodou conflict

An account of a man who demonized Vodou in order to cover up his murderous actions. He, along with many others, hold Christianity above Vodou and make it seem as if Vodou is evil and Christianity is above all. This article is definitely skewed to make us feel bad about Vodouisants and have us think that Christianity and Vodou can work together in harmony.

Statue of Mary defaced

The face of a statue of Mary was spray painted and a sign that read a Vodou goddess's name (Erzulie) was taped to the back of it. Caused upset amoung Catholics.

Timeline of diffusion and growth

A fact based timeline that contains information on the origins of Vodou and beliefs, as well as diffusion and development of it.

Top 10 important historical figures for Vodou

An article containing information about famous historical Vodouisants. Unbiased because no personal opinions are given.

Vodou handbook

This book contains base information on Vodou and how to practice it, as well as morals and rituals. Contains solid facts instead of embellishments in order to make it sound more interesting.

Origin history

This article contains informatin regarding the origins of Vodou. It contains mostly facts and information, instead of opinions or inside sources.

Religion Explained

Veves and Loas explained

A fact based online article discussing the different Vodou symbols and explaining the connection between Vodou religious practices and Loas, or spirits.

Bondye

An online article describing history, beliefs and traditions of Vodou and discussing Bondye, the Vodou God, from the perspective of a Vodou Priest.

Initiation Facts

Factual description of ordination in Vodou and initiation facts.

Initiation first hand

A personal blog describing fascination with firsthand Vodou initiation experiences. This leans extremely towards Vodou and the positive aspects of it; it was written by a Vodouisant

Prayers

Fact based information and analyzation of The Priye Ginen which is the Prayer of Africa.

Loas in depth

A fact based online article containing detailed information about the Vodou spirits, Loas

Purpose of life

An online resource that entails the essence of Vodou and it’s fight to survive.

Fundamental beliefs

An online article discussing the fundamental beliefs of Vodou and why it’s perceived as “scary.”

Morals

An online resource describing different Vodou morals and perspectives and discussing the ethicality of Vodou.

Basic structure

An online website that entails the structure of Vodou, as well as its history and origination and persecution.

Holidays

An online resources that dates different Vodou holidays and describes them.

Hayti or the Black Republic

A book written in 1884 to demonize and criticize Vodou religious practices. This was one of the first books to persuade others into believing that Vodou is “evil” and “witchcraft.”
